Definition of the department

Otorhinolaryngology is a head subspecialty in medicine that deals with conditions of the ear, nose, throat, and related organs in the neck. Doctors who specialize in this field are called otolaryngologists or surgeons, or head and neck surgeons. Patients seek an otolaryngologist for the treatment of diseases of the ear, nose, throat, and base, and for the management of cancers, and polyps of the head and neck.

People with ear, nose, throat and head and neck conditions are cared for by physicians who specialize in the Ear, Nose, Throat and Head and Neck Surgery Departments at Mayo Clinic. The clinic has one of the largest ear, nose and throat practices in the world, with more than 50 specialists committed to providing personalized medical and surgical care to people at its centers in Arizona, Minnesota and Florida. Mayo Clinic Health System campuses serve more than 60 communities in Iowa, Wisconsin and Minnesota. At these locations, Mayo Clinic offers its extensive expertise in otolaryngology and head and neck surgery for the benefit of the local communities in which it is located.
